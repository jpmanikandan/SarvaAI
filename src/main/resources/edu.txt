Sarva Gurukul EdTech Guidance

1. Learning Methodology: Focus on first-principles thinking. Break complex problems into smaller, manageable parts before attempting to solve them.
2. Physics - Newton's Laws: 
   - First Law: An object stays at rest or in motion unless acted upon by an external force.
   - Second Law: Force equals mass times acceleration (F=ma).
   - Third Law: For every action, there is an equal and opposite reaction.
3. Mathematics - Study Tips: Practice regularly. Understand the 'why' behind formulas rather than just memorizing them.
4. Computer Science: Start with logic and algorithms. Language is just a tool; the core is problem-solving.
5. Soft Skills: Effective communication and time management are as important as technical knowledge.
6. Multi-language Learning: Use Sarva Bhasha to translate concepts into your native language to grasp difficult topics faster.

---
English Learning Module (Supernova AI Style)

7. Grammar Rules:
   - Subject-Verb Agreement: A singular subject takes a singular verb, while a plural subject takes a plural verb. Example: "She eats" vs "They eat".
   - Tenses: Use Present Simple for habits ("I study"), Present Continuous for actions happening now ("I am studying").
   - Articles: Use 'a' before consonants, 'an' before vowels, and 'the' for specific things.

8. Conversation Practice Scripts:
   - Restaurant: "I would like to order a steak." "Could I have the bill, please?"
   - Job Interview: "Tell me about yourself." "What are your strengths?"
   - Small Talk: "Nice weather today, isn't it?" "What do you do for a living?"

9. Common Idioms & Phrasal Verbs:
   - "Break the ice": Starting a conversation.
   - "Call it a day": Deciding to stop working for the day.
   - "Look up to": To respect someone.

10. Native Language Bridging: If you don't understand a term like "Preposition", ask: "Explain Prepositions in my native language."
